# Luxury Rules

## Purpose

Define the luxury design principles that keep Bhalow creatives premium, refined, and suitable for the US fragrance market.

## Core Principle

Luxury is created through restraint, quality, space, lighting, and clarity. It is not created by adding more objects.

## Luxury Signals

Use:
- Generous negative space
- Premium lighting
- Realistic glass reflections
- Marble, crystal, silk, metal, water, and soft mist
- Clean typography
- Balanced composition
- Limited color palette
- Product-first hierarchy

## Restraint Rules

- Use fewer elements with better quality.
- Avoid crowding the design.
- Avoid too many fonts, colors, badges, or props.
- Leave room for the product to breathe.

## Premium Material Rules

Preferred materials:
- White marble
- Champagne glass
- Crystal
- Polished metal
- Silk fabric
- Dark stone
- Clear water
- Soft petals

Avoid cheap-looking materials:
- Plastic-looking props
- Low-quality textures
- Fake glitter overload
- Random stock-photo backgrounds

## Copy Luxury Rules

- Keep copy concise.
- Avoid shouting.
- Avoid heavy discount language unless requested.
- Use premium words such as discover, curated, authentic, signature scent, and timeless elegance.

## Visual Luxury Rules

- Product must be sharp.
- Background must be controlled.
- Lighting must be soft and believable.
- Typography must be readable and elegant.

## Avoid

- Loud sale stickers
- Chaotic backgrounds
- Oversaturated colors
- Too many decorative effects
- Unrealistic bottle distortion
- Busy marketplace collage style

## Final Rule

If the design feels loud, cheap, or cluttered, remove elements, increase spacing, simplify color, and strengthen the product hero.
