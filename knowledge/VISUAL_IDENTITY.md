# Visual Identity

## Purpose

This file defines the visual standards for Bhalow creative work. These rules apply to website thumbnails, social media posters, Facebook covers, hero banners, email banners, CGI ads, and any AI-generated campaign visuals.

## Visual Personality

Bhalow visuals should communicate:

- Premium fragrance retail
- Clean luxury
- Modern editorial beauty
- Authenticity and trust
- Refined product presentation
- US-market e-commerce polish

## Color System

### Primary Colors

Use these as the default Bhalow visual foundation:

- Ivory
- Warm white
- Champagne
- Soft gold
- Crystal white

### Secondary Colors

Use these to support product mood and campaign direction:

- Crystal silver
- Deep black
- Emerald
- Soft rose
- Warm taupe
- Pale blush
- Amber gold

### Accent Effects

Accent should come from lighting and materials, not heavy graphic blocks:

- Glass reflections
- Soft golden particles
- Luxury mist
- Subtle bokeh
- Crystal highlights
- Metallic cap reflections

## Color Behavior

Bhalow should not look overly colorful. The product packaging and fragrance liquid should provide much of the color. Backgrounds should stay refined and supportive.

## Logo Usage Rules

When the official Bhalow logo is uploaded:

- Use the uploaded logo exactly as provided.
- Never redesign the logo.
- Never recolor the logo.
- Never stretch or squash the logo.
- Never crop the logo.
- Never simplify or recreate the logo.
- Preserve original proportions and transparency.
- Keep generous breathing space around it.
- Place it where it is visible but not overpowering.

For Facebook covers, the logo usually belongs in the upper-right safe area.

For social posters, the logo may sit at the top or bottom depending on the layout.

For website thumbnails, do not include the logo unless the user specifically requests it.

## Typography Direction

### Headline Style

Use elegant serif or premium editorial-style typography for high-end fragrance campaigns.

Headline should feel:

- Elegant
- Readable
- Refined
- Premium
- Calm

### Supporting Text Style

Use modern sans-serif styling for:

- Product details
- Notes
- Feature lines
- CTA support
- Website URL

### CTA Style

CTA should be clean and premium. Preferred CTA examples:

- Shop Now
- Explore Collection
- Discover More
- Find Your Signature Scent
- View Collection
- Shop Authentic Fragrances

Avoid cheap-looking sale buttons unless it is a sale campaign.

## Layout Principles

Every layout should prioritize:

1. Product visibility
2. Clean hierarchy
3. Negative space
4. Readable typography
5. Mobile safety
6. Premium balance

## Spacing Rules

Luxury depends on breathing space. Avoid crowding product, logo, copy, and CTA together.

General spacing guidance:

- Use generous margins.
- Keep logo away from edges.
- Separate headline, subheading, CTA, and product with clear visual hierarchy.
- Avoid dense feature lists unless needed.

## Design Density

Bhalow should feel curated, not crowded.

Use fewer elements with better lighting, better spacing, and better material detail.

## Image Style

Preferred visual style:

- Ultra-realistic product photography
- Premium CGI when appropriate
- Physically believable glass
- Realistic liquid colors
- Soft shadows
- Natural reflections
- Luxury beauty lighting
- High-end editorial composition

Avoid:

- Cartoon-like product rendering
- Overly artificial backgrounds
- Harsh neon colors
- Cheap ecommerce collage style
- Too many badges
- Unreadable typography

## Button Style

Buttons should feel like premium UI elements:

- Rounded or softly rounded corners
- Clean typography
- Subtle glass or solid fill
- Minimal shadow
- No aggressive outline unless needed

## Visual Hierarchy

Typical hierarchy for a social poster:

1. Product hero
2. Headline
3. Key product benefit or fragrance notes
4. CTA
5. Website
6. Logo

Typical hierarchy for Facebook cover:

1. Hero composition
2. Headline
3. Supporting copy
4. Logo
5. Website
6. CTA

Typical hierarchy for website thumbnail:

1. Product only
2. Shadow and reflection
3. No text

## Final Rule

If a design does not feel premium, reduce the number of elements, increase spacing, improve lighting, and make the product stronger.
