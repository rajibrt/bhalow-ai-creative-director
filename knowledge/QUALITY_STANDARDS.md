# Quality Standards

## Purpose

This file defines the quality control system for Bhalow AI Creative Director. The GPT should use these standards before delivering image prompts, copy, creative concepts, and campaign directions.

The goal is to ensure every Bhalow output feels premium, accurate, US-market-ready, and consistent with the brand.

## Quality Philosophy

A Bhalow creative should look like it belongs to a premium US beauty retailer. It should be polished, product-first, readable, mobile-aware, and commercially usable.

Quality comes from:

- Strong product focus
- Clean composition
- Accurate product details
- Premium lighting
- Readable typography
- Brand consistency
- Visual restraint
- Trustworthy copy

## Universal Quality Checklist

Before final output, check:

- Is the product the hero?
- Is the visual direction aligned with Bhalow?
- Is the target market clearly US-focused?
- Is the language US English?
- Is the copy premium and not cheap?
- Is the prompt concise and not overloaded?
- Are logo rules included when a logo is used?
- Is the layout safe for the requested platform?
- Are unsupported claims avoided?

## Product Quality Standards

For product visuals, ensure:

- Product proportions remain believable.
- Bottle cap, label, and packaging are not distorted.
- The bottle is not cropped unless intentionally requested.
- Product label remains as readable as possible.
- Glass and liquid look realistic.
- Reflections and shadows are physically believable.
- Props do not cover the product name.
- Background does not compete with the product.

## Logo Quality Standards

When using the official Bhalow logo:

- Use the uploaded logo exactly as provided.
- Do not redesign, recolor, crop, stretch, rotate, simplify, or recreate it.
- Keep generous safe space around the logo.
- Make sure the logo has enough contrast.
- Keep the logo secondary to the product unless the design is brand-level.

For Facebook covers:

- Logo usually belongs in the upper-right safe area.
- Website can sit below the logo.
- Avoid placing logo too close to the edge.

## Typography Quality Standards

Typography must be:

- Readable
- Clean
- Premium
- Properly spaced
- Platform appropriate
- Not distorted
- Not overlapping the product

Avoid:

- Too many font styles
- Text over busy areas
- Long paragraphs on images
- Extremely small copy
- Overlapping typography
- Low-contrast text

## Platform-Specific Quality Standards

### Website Thumbnail

Must include:

- 1:1 ratio
- Product centered
- White or clean background
- No text
- No logo unless requested
- Soft shadow
- Natural reflection only if helpful
- E-commerce-ready composition

Must avoid:

- Marketing copy
- Decorative backgrounds
- Heavy props
- Cropped product
- Wrong product angle

### Social Poster

Must include when appropriate:

- Product hero
- Headline
- Subheading or feature line
- CTA
- Bhalow logo if provided
- Website: www.bhalow.com

Must avoid:

- Too much text
- Cluttered props
- Cheap sale stickers
- Text too close to edge
- Product covered by copy

### Facebook Cover

Must include:

- 1640 x 624 composition guidance
- Mobile-safe central layout
- Left negative space for copy when needed
- Right safe space for logo and website
- Wide premium hero composition

Must avoid:

- Important product details near edges
- Logo too close to border
- Dense copy
- Overcrowded product arrangements
- Small unreadable text

### Website Hero Banner

Must include:

- Wide premium layout
- Clear hero area
- Strong CTA
- Clean negative space
- Landing-page-ready structure

Must avoid:

- Cropped product
- Too much copy
- Too many competing products
- Background overpowering the product

### CGI Advertisement

Must include:

- Concept clarity
- Product-first realism
- Believable materials
- Cinematic but controlled lighting
- Premium commercial quality

Must avoid:

- Visual effects covering the product
- Unrealistic product deformation
- Excessive particles
- Overly chaotic fantasy scenes

## Copy Quality Standards

Copy should be:

- US English
- Premium
- Clear
- Concise
- Honest
- Benefit-driven
- Appropriate for the platform

Avoid unsupported claims such as:

- Guaranteed compliments
- Best in the world
- Official authorized seller unless verified
- Lowest price unless proven
- Long-lasting unless provided or sourced

## Prompt Quality Standards

A strong image prompt should include:

1. Design type and canvas
2. Product role
3. Brand context
4. Background
5. Composition
6. Lighting
7. Materials
8. Typography instructions, if needed
9. Logo and website rules, if needed
10. Quality and constraints

A prompt should not:

- Repeat the same idea too many times
- Include contradictory style directions
- Be unnecessarily long
- Ask for too many unrelated props
- Mix cheap sales language with luxury art direction

## Luxury Scorecard

When reviewing a creative direction, mentally score it from 1 to 10:

- Product focus
- Lighting quality
- Composition
- Brand consistency
- Typography readability
- Luxury feel
- US-market suitability
- Prompt clarity

If any area is below 7, revise before final output.

## Common Failure Fixes

### If the design feels cheap

Reduce elements, remove aggressive sales language, add negative space, improve lighting, use better materials.

### If the design feels cluttered

Remove props, simplify background, shorten copy, increase spacing.

### If the product is weak

Make the product larger, improve contrast, reduce background detail, use hero lighting.

### If typography is unreadable

Move text to negative space, increase contrast, shorten copy, use cleaner hierarchy.

### If the prompt is failing

Shorten the prompt, remove repeated quality terms, simplify composition, reduce the number of objects.

## Final Rule

If the output does not feel like a premium US fragrance retailer, revise the strategy before delivering the final prompt.
